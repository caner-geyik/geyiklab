Research Interests

1. The Role of tsRNA in Cellular Stress

Transfer RNA-derived small RNAs (tsRNAs) are not merely degradation products but are functional small non-coding RNAs. Our lab investigates how specific tsRNA species are upregulated during oxidative stress conditions.

We utilize RNA-sequencing to map these fragments and determine their targets within the cell.

2. Nrf2 Pathway Regulation

The Nrf2 antioxidant response element is crucial for cell survival. We are studying the interaction between non-coding RNAs and Nrf2 signaling.

Our current focus is on how this interaction influences drug resistance in cancer models.

3. Temozolomide Resistance Mechanisms

Glioblastoma remains difficult to treat due to resistance to alkylating agents like Temozolomide. We are exploring novel molecular pathways that confer this resistance, looking for potential targets for combination therapy.
